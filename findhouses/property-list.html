Best Places to Live in Bangalore

The ever-expanding chic city of Bangalore offers residential flats in different ranges. You can find individual flats in Bangalore in a range of less than Rs 5,000 per sqft to those above Rs 7,500 per sqft. You can find flats in posh localities and in more modest areas too.


Bangalore has seen a huge expansion in the real estate market for flats in the last 2-3 decades. This trend had seen stagnation for the two-three years of the pandemic. But the market has picked up again and the growth trends visible in the previous decades are expected to continue in the future.


If you are planning to buy a flat in Bangalore, here are some areas to choose from:

1 - Kanakapura Road Vakil Garden City

If you are looking for a flat in Bangalore that comes with the benefit of both a serene environment and proximity to IT hubs, then Kanakapura Road is the best. The area is home to a number of high-end residential projects equipped with all the modern amenities. This includes Godrej Eternity, Mantri Tranquil, Prestige Falcon City, and Brigade Meadows, to name a few. Excellent metro connectivity and availability of Cauvery water further make this locality more favorable. Kanakapura Road is also well-connected to Banashankari, Outer Ring Road, Bannerghatta Road, Electronic City, and Jayanagar. The demand for flats in Bangalore on Kanakapura Road has been fueled by the presence of numerous well-established schools, top hospitals, and well-known shopping malls nearby.

About Bangalore

Bangalore, the famed ‘Silicon Valley of India’ is a rapidly expanding and extremely popular city. Being the capital city of Karnataka, Bangalore has the right amenities to attract people from all over the country. This smart city has smart infrastructure, and it also has hills, gardens, and lakes adding to its charm. The cosmopolitan atmosphere and the modern vibe of the city are inviting for outsiders. It often hosts mega National and International events, that enhance its image as a vibrant cosmopolitan. The city is also the origin of many new enterprises, start-ups and innovative missions.

Since it is a major industrial, economic and educational hub, Bangalore has always had a boomingreal estate market in India. Well-educated people and rising entrepreneurs residing in the cityexpect a certain standard of lifestyle. The demand for great amenities and convenient facilitiesis always high. As a result, new-age townships with state-of-the-art flats/apartmets have seenan increase. The growing demands need to go hand-in-hand with the development of infrastructure.This makes the real estate market quite competitive.

The good news is that the city has different price ranges for residential flats in different localities based on the amenities they provide. Whether you want individual flats/apartments, gated row flats or luxury flats in Bangalore, you will definitely find something to fit your budget here.

Bangalore Real Estate Market

The real estate market in Bangalore is seeing high growth in all aspects – demand, supply and property rates. Flats in Bangalore are seeing a decline in vacancies which means that there is more demand for occupancy. This rise is reflecting growth in the leasing of office spaces. After almost 2 years of a stagnant market (during Covid pandemic), this change is due to returning employees and students. The property market in Bangalore alone is witnessing a growth of 18.8% in the 2nd quarter of 2022. Major developers are coming up with appealing projects and attractive flat options.

The eastern part of the city is more preferred by buyers. You will find some of the localities in Eastern Bangalore such as Whitefield and Kanakapura Road better connected to the employment hubs. The city also sees a larger preference for 3 BHK flats as compared to 2 BHK flats in Bangalore. The slight mismatch in this segment is 49% demand and 43% supply making it clear why the prices are witnessing this high. Apartments in Bangalore in places like Electronic City and Indira Nagar have a higher demand for rentals. On the other hand, property rates in Hosur Road, HSR Layout and Marathahalli-Sarjapur Road are seeing a rise of about 4-6%.

Some localities with affordable rates for flats, i.e. less than Rs 5000 per square foot are – Electronic City, Kengeri, Hoskote, Chandrapura, Attibele, Gunjur, Rajarajeshwar, etc.

In the medium range of Rs 5000-7500 per square foot, you will find localities like – Hosur Road, HSR Layout, Sarjapur Road, K R Puram, Bellary Road, Vijaynagar, Budigere etc.

On the high range i.e. above Rs 7500 per square foot, you will find posh localities of Rajaji Nagar, Koramangala, Yeshwanthpur, Hebbal Kempapura, Indira Nagar, Jayanagar, Basavanagudi, Malleshwaram etc.
